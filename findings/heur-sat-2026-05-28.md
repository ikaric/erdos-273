## TL;DR

The bounded-period strict-covering question — *is there a covering
of $\mathbb{Z}/L\mathbb{Z}$ by progressions whose moduli are distinct
elements of $\mathcal{M} = \{p-1 : p \text{ prime}, p \geq 5\}$ that
all divide $L$?* — is **unsatisfiable** for every $L$ in the canonical
sequence $\{60, 120, 360, 840, 2520, 27720\}$, both by a
constant-time density (counting) argument and (for $L \leq 840$)
confirmed independently by the CaDiCaL SAT solver. For
$L \in \{360360, 720720\}$ the density argument no longer applies
($D(L) > 1$); both kissat and CaDiCaL fail to settle the question
within reasonable timeouts. **The Erdős #273 conjecture is therefore
strengthened numerically as follows:** any strict covering of
$\mathbb{Z}$ by progressions with moduli in $\mathcal{M}$ must use
some modulus $m$ with $m \nmid 27720$.

## What this run computes

For each fixed period $L$, the script
`formal/numerics/sat_covering.py` encodes the following decision
problem as CNF and dispatches it to CaDiCaL (or kissat) via DIMACS
subprocess:

> Choose a subset $S \subseteq \{(a, m) : m \in \mathcal{M},\ m \mid L,\ 0 \leq a < m\}$
> such that:
> 1. *Cover:* every residue $i \in [0, L)$ has some $(a, m) \in S$
>    with $i \equiv a \pmod{m}$;
> 2. *Strict:* for every $m$, at most one residue is paired with $m$
>    (Erdős–Graham distinct-moduli convention, matching the
>    `StrictCoveringSystem` type in DeepMind formal-conjectures
>    `FormalConjecturesForMathlib/NumberTheory/CoveringSystem.lean`).

If SAT, the chosen pairs lift to a strict covering of $\mathbb{Z}$
(each progression with $m \mid L$ is $L$-periodic, so covering
$\mathbb{Z}/L\mathbb{Z}$ ≡ covering $\mathbb{Z}$). This would settle
Erdős #273 affirmatively, with an explicit construction.

If UNSAT, no such covering with all moduli dividing $L$ exists. So
any (hypothetical) Erdős #273 covering must use a modulus not
dividing $L$; equivalently, its LCM is not a divisor of $L$. Each
fresh UNSAT $L$ strengthens this LCM lower bound.

### Density (counting) certificate

Independently of SAT, the strict-covering version admits a
**one-line counting upper bound**:

> If $S$ is a strict covering of $\mathbb{Z}/L\mathbb{Z}$ using
> moduli in $\mathcal{M} \cap \mathrm{div}(L)$ with each modulus used
> at most once, then
> $$L \leq \sum_{(a,m)\in S} (L/m) \leq L \cdot \sum_{m \in \mathcal{M},\ m\mid L} \frac{1}{m} =: L \cdot D(L).$$
> So $D(L) < 1 \Rightarrow$ UNSAT.

This holds because each progression $a \pmod m$ covers exactly
$L/m$ residues of $[0, L)$ when $m \mid L$, and the chosen moduli
are pairwise distinct.

## Lower bound for Erdős #273

Combining the UNSAT results for $L \in \{60, 120, 360, 840, 2520,
27720\}$ — noting that $60, 120, 360, 840, 2520$ all divide $27720$,
so $L = 27720$ subsumes the smaller values — gives the headline
inequality:

> **Numerical lower bound.** If a strict covering of $\mathbb{Z}$
> exists with every modulus in $\mathcal{M} = \{p - 1 : p$ prime,
> $p \geq 5\}$, then at least one of its moduli does **not divide
> $27720 = 2^3 \cdot 3^2 \cdot 5 \cdot 7 \cdot 11$**.

This is the deliverable's `[numerical: $L \leq 27720$]` manuscript
claim. (Equivalently: the LCM of the moduli of any such covering
must be divisible by at least one prime power outside $\{8, 9, 5,
7, 11\}$.)

For $L \in \{360360, 720720\}$ neither the density bound nor a CDCL
solver at the timeouts attempted settles the question. The first
prime powers introduced are $13$ (at $L = 360360 = 27720 \cdot 13$)
and $16, 13$ (at $L = 720720 = 27720 \cdot 26$). Larger CDCL budgets
or a structural argument are needed to extend the lower bound.

## Why the SAT solver times out on UNSAT instances at $L \geq 2520$

The density argument is **substantially stronger than what CDCL
proof systems (resolution + clause learning) can derive efficiently**
for the strict-covering CNF. At $L = 2520$, $D(L) = 0.872$: even a
proof of size $O(L)$ exists (just point to any uncovered residue
class density-deficit), but CDCL has to derive UNSAT clause-by-clause
through resolution, which appears to be exponential here. CaDiCaL
exhausts a 30+ s budget and still does not return.

This is a known pathology of CDCL on combinatorial pigeonhole-like
instances; it underscores the value of the density argument as
both (a) a sound certificate of UNSAT and (b) a much smaller
proof artifact for downstream formalization (the Lean proof of
"$\sum_{m \in S} 1/m < 1 \Rightarrow$ no cover" is ~5 lines, much
shorter than any LRAT certificate).

## Surprises / notable observations

1. **The density bound saturates at $L = 27720$.** $D(27720) =
   0.9626$, just barely below 1. The next canonical $L$ (= $360360$
   = $27720 \cdot 13$) introduces the modulus $52 = 53 - 1$,
   $78 = 79 - 1$, $156$, etc., and pushes $D$ over 1. Heuristically,
   the density $D(L)$ tracks $\sum_{p \leq P, p\,\text{prime}} \chi_{\{p \mid L+1\}}/(p-1)$,
   and the partial Mertens-product behavior dictates when it
   crosses 1. **The deeper number-theoretic content of #273 is
   that $D(L) \geq 1$ for $L$ divisible by sufficiently many
   small-prime-power factors, yet the *structural* covering
   constraints (residue compatibility across moduli) still rule
   out a solution — this is exactly where #273's open status
   lives.**
2. **The 4-fold mod-4 "covering" is excluded by strict-mode.** Under
   the literal "moduli $\in \mathcal{M}$" reading without
   distinctness, $\{0, 1, 2, 3\} \pmod 4$ trivially covers
   $\mathbb{Z}$ (4 ∈ $\mathcal{M}$ since $5$ is prime). The
   $p \geq 5$ restriction is only meaningful under the strict
   (Erdős–Graham distinct-moduli) convention. The script enforces
   this via per-modulus at-most-one CNF constraints.
3. **`ladder` AMO encoding is ~300× faster to construct than
   `seqcounter`.** For $L = 360360$, switching from pysat's
   sequential-counter AMO to `EncType.ladder` reduced CNF
   construction from 77 s to 0.14 s (per modulus $m = 180180$).
   Recorded in the script as `_PAIRWISE_AMO_THRESHOLD = 64` plus
   `EncType.ladder` for larger groups.
4. **Standalone-cadical subprocess timeout is reliable; pysat's
   in-process `interrupt()` is not.** Initial implementations using
   pysat's `solve_limited(expect_interrupt=True)` left the cadical
   C-library stuck past the timeout. Refactor to DIMACS-on-disk +
   `subprocess.run(..., timeout=...)` made wall-clock enforcement
   deterministic.

## Open questions surfaced

- **Q1.** Push CDCL or a tailored CSP search to settle $L \in
  \{360360, 720720\}$. Current CaDiCaL / kissat budgets (60 s, 600 s)
  fail. A custom backtracking with density-bound pruning may
  succeed where CDCL doesn't.
- **Q2.** Find the smallest $L$ with $D(L) \geq 1$. The canonical
  list has $L = 360360$ as the first such $L$, but smaller
  $\mathcal{M}$-rich $L$ (e.g., $L$'s with more small $p-1$ divisors
  than 27720) may exist. Pre-screen candidates by $D(L)$.
- **Q3.** Connect the empirical $D(L) < 1$ regime to BBMST's
  density theorem (lit-covering-2026-05-28.md §2.1): is there a
  closed-form $\sup_L D(L)$, or does $D(L) \to \infty$ as $L$ ranges
  over $\mathrm{lcm}\{p_i - 1 : p_i \leq P\}$? If $D(L) \to \infty$,
  pure counting will fail for large $L$, and the structural
  argument (BBMST distortion) becomes essential.

## Wiring for Lean

The density certificate $D(L) < 1 \Rightarrow$ UNSAT can be
formalized in Lean essentially verbatim:

```
-- Pseudocode for the formal statement.
theorem strict_covering_density_bound
    (L : ℕ) (S : Finset (ℕ × ℕ))
    (h_mods : ∀ p ∈ S, p.2 ∈ ℳ ∧ p.2 ∣ L ∧ p.1 < p.2)
    (h_distinct : S.toList.map Prod.snd |>.Nodup)
    (h_cover : ∀ i < L, ∃ p ∈ S, i % p.2 = p.1) :
    (1 : ℝ) ≤ ∑ p ∈ S, (1 : ℝ) / p.2 := by
  -- each (a, m) covers exactly L/m residues; sum >= L; divide by L.
  sorry  -- formalist V1.next
```

This is the single Lean lemma that connects the SAT/density
evidence to the manuscript's `[numerical: $L \leq 27720$]` claim.
With it formalized, the per-$L$ unsatisfiability conclusion at
$L \in \{60, …, 27720\}$ becomes an `Erdos273.UnsatAtL.<L>`
theorem whose proof is just `norm_num` (to compute $D(L)$) plus
the density lemma. **This is the next-session vector for the
formalist** — short proof, high-value entry into the manuscript.

(See `findings/lit-covering-2026-05-28.md` §3.4 and §5 for context:
the Klein–Koukoulopoulos–Lemieux 2024 result implies that any
covering with all moduli $\geq 4$ has $\sum 1/d_i$ bounded away
from 1; their constant is not explicit, but our $D(L)$ values for
$L \leq 27720$ stay below $0.97$, consistent with their qualitative
bound.)
